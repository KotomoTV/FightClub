04 · Sparring Game Plans

The Playbook

Pier
Power Kicker · Vs. The Others
Vs. Arno
End it early
Arno has the cardio (8) and feints (6) to outlast and outthink you. Every round you give him, he grows. Detonate the legs in round one and force a clinch war where your strength (7) takes over.
  • Heavy low kicks in the first exchange
  • Clinch when he loads a middle kick
  • Don't chase feints, stay planted and counter
  • Cut off the ring, deny him space to circle
Vs. Eric
Stay at kicking range
Eric wants to close and box (7). Your boxing (3) is the weak link. Keep him at leg-kick range and punish his entries. His cardio (3) breaks before yours, so extend exchanges.
  • Teep any forward pressure, reset range
  • Low kick on his jab to break rhythm
  • If he closes, clinch hard, don't box back
  • Force round two, his cardio breaks
Vs. Axel
Teach, don't hunt
You outclass the prospect everywhere except cardio (5 vs 7). This is a working round, not a war. Drill your tools at controlled intensity. Don't blow out his knees with low kicks just because you can.
  • Low kicks at 50% power, full technique
  • Let him feel the clinch, don't crush him
  • Reset often, give him clean reps
  • Respect the engine, he'll push your pace
Vs. Guil
Pure teach mode
Biggest gap on the card. Nothing to prove. Guil has cardio (7) and almost nothing else (1s and 2s). Work on placement and rhythm, never land flush. Set the tone for how you want prospects to learn on this mat.
  • Pull every kick, placement over power
  • Let him initiate, react clean
  • Zero hard clinch, no throws
  • Reset often, build his confidence
Arno
Technical Endurance · Vs. The Others
Vs. Pier
Move, check, outlast
Pier is slower (3) and one-dimensional. His only real threats are low kicks and clinch strength. Neutralize both by staying mobile, checking kicks, and refusing to clinch. Your cardio wins this in deep water.
  • Check every low kick on the shin
  • Angle off, never stay square in front
  • Mid kicks to the body, attack his cardio
  • Refuse clinch, break or circle out
Vs. Eric
Keep him on kicks
Eric is dangerous in the pocket (boxing 7, pressure 6) but weak at range and gassed quickly (cardio 3). Don't let him close. Teep, mid kick, feint, pivot. Stretch the round and his engine dies.
  • Teep to the hip on every step in
  • Feint the jab, land mid kick to liver
  • Never trade in the pocket, pivot out
  • Extend rounds, let his cardio collapse
Vs. Axel
Become the teacher
You're the benchmark he's chasing. This is his most valuable round of the day. Slow everything down so he can read it. Show him the feint, then the kick. Reward good defense by giving him openings.
  • Telegraph feints slowly, let him read them
  • Mid kicks at 40%, check his guard
  • Let him land when his technique is right
  • Talk after the round, not during
Vs. Guil
Teach the foundation
He's at Axel's level minus a year. Every technique you show is new vocabulary. Slow it all the way down, name what you do. Reward defense. Your job isn't to score, it's to build his pattern recognition from zero.
  • Telegraph each feint, let him see it coming
  • Mid kicks at 30%, land clean on the guard
  • Wait for his offense, give him the opening
  • Name the move after the round
Eric
Pressure Boxer · Vs. The Others
Vs. Pier
Close and smother
Pier is slow (3) and his kicks need space. Rob him of both. Step inside on every kick attempt, box to the body, and tie up in the clinch. Your boxing (7) is a four-point gap over his (3).
  • Step inside his low kick, don't back up
  • Body-head combos in the pocket
  • Clinch and reset when he loads
  • Check or catch, never absorb the low kick
Vs. Arno
Pressure, kill the feints
Toughest fight on the card. Arno outclasses you in cardio, speed, and feints. Your only path is to be the aggressor from second one. Press, cut the ring, make him fight backing up where his setups fall apart.
  • Walk him down, never give a free step back
  • Close on his feints, don't react
  • Jab-cross-hook into clinch to drain his gas
  • Commit early, cardio won't hold round 3
Vs. Axel
Controlled pocket work
Big mismatch on paper: boxing 7 vs 2, pressure 6 vs 2. This is rep work for you and defense school for him. Pressure enough for him to feel it, pull punches so he can learn to slip instead of panic.
  • Forward pressure at 70% intensity
  • Clean combinations, no head hunting
  • Let him tee off when you over-commit
  • Watch your cardio, he'll make you work
Vs. Guil
Pressure school
Biggest boxing gap on the card (7 vs 2) and pressure gap (6 vs 1). He can't defend combinations yet. Use this to drill your combos cleanly and leave him space to react. Half power, full form: you're rebuilding his reflexes, not testing them.
  • Combinations at 50% power, full form
  • Pause after each set, let him reset
  • Never chain more than 3 shots
  • Ask him to slip, not block
Axel
The Prospect · Vs. The Vets
Vs. Pier
Survive the legs, stay long
Hardest physical test. His low kick (7) and strength (7) will punish any mistake. Use your cardio edge (7 vs 5): keep moving, refuse to clinch, make him chase. Every minute you survive is a minute of gold.
  • Stay outside his kick range, circle constantly
  • Check low kicks, don't block with the thigh
  • Never clinch, he'll manhandle you
  • Use your engine, keep the pace high
Vs. Arno
The master class
Most valuable round of your week. You can't beat him, but you can absorb. Watch his feints, time his mid kicks, feel how he sets up shots. Your cardio (7) is close to his (8), so bank real rounds with him, not survival ones.
  • Stay engaged, don't shell up
  • Read his hips before the kick lands
  • Try one feint of your own per exchange
  • Ask questions between rounds, not during
Vs. Eric
Distance management 101
Eric wants to close; you need space to survive. This is pure footwork practice. Every time he enters, you need an answer: teep, angle, or circle. His cardio (3) is your ally if you can last the opening minute.
  • Teep on every forward step, even basic
  • Angle, don't back straight
  • Don't trade in the pocket, you lose
  • Wait him out, his gas tank empties fast
Vs. Guil
Big brother round
Prospect vs prospect, one year apart. You hold the edge on every technique stat (3s where he has 2s) and on pressure (2 vs 1). Don't lord it over, just show what the same engine looks like with a few more reps. Help him feel the gap he needs to close.
  • Share the pace, same engine league
  • Show clean technique, never power
  • Call out what worked between rounds
  • Remember you were here a year ago
Guil
The Newcomer · Vs. The Room
Vs. Pier
Survive, don't engage
Hardest physical test on the card. His low kick (7) and strength (7) will punish every mistake, and you don't have the tools to answer. Cardio (7) is the only card you can play: keep moving, refuse the clinch, make him chase. Every minute survived is a win.
  • Stay outside his kick range, circle constantly
  • Check low kicks, don't eat them on the thigh
  • Never clinch, he'll manhandle you
  • Use your engine, keep the pace high
Vs. Arno
Absorb, don't compete
Most educational round you'll get this week. You can't win a technical exchange against the benchmark, so don't try. Use it as film in real time: watch his hips before the kick, his hands before the feint. Your cardio (7) keeps you in the round long enough to learn.
  • Stay engaged, don't shell up
  • Read his hip before the kick lands
  • Don't force offense, just react
  • Ask specific questions after, not during
Vs. Eric
Distance or die
Eric wants the pocket, and the pocket is a grave for you (boxing 2, pressure response 1). Deny every entry. Circle, teep, anything that breaks his line. Cardio (7 vs 3) is the only real asset you have, so stretch the round past minute one.
  • Never back straight, always angle
  • Teep or push kick on every entry
  • Don't trade hands, tie up and reset
  • Buy time, his cardio dies round one
Vs. Axel
Prospect drill
Mirror match. Both of you are raw, and he's one rung up (3s to your 2s, a year of reps in the bank). No ego needed, this is the one round where neither of you risks embarrassment. Use it to work the basics at matched pace and ask him what he's learned.
  • Stay middle distance, work the jab
  • Throw a kick on every reset
  • Breathe between exchanges
  • Watch how another novice moves
